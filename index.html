I am a PhD student at the Oxford Robotics Institute at the University of Oxford as part of the GOALS group, supervised by Prof. Nick Hawes.

I'm interested in real-time robot motion planning and risk-aware methods for robot autonomy under uncertainty. My research spans across the fields of probabilistic planning, control, optimization and learning. It includes the development of novel methods for robot motion planning and control that enable robots to operate safely in complex and dynamic environments with humans while still being task-efficient.

A model-based optimization approach that synthesizes bi-manual pushing trajectories by controlling the variance of the object configuration, without explicitly modeling contact modes. Difficulty-Aware Time-Bounded Planning under Uncertainty for Large-Scale Robot Missions
M. Staniaszek*, L. Bruderm√ºller*, R. Bhattacharyya, B. Lacerda, N. Hawes
Autonomous Robots and Multirobot Systems (ARMS) Workshop @ AAMAS , 2023
Selected as best workshop paper

This work extends our previous work on time-bounded large-scale mission planning. Our focus is problems where the difficulty of each task, and thus its duration, can be predicted, but not fully known in advance. We propose a general MDP model for difficulty-aware problems, including variants on this model which allow adaptation to different robotics domains.

Time-bounded large-scale mission planning under uncertainty for UV disinfection
L. Bruderm√ºller*, R. Bhattacharyya, B. Lacerda, N. Hawes
PlanRob workshop @ ICAPS, 2022

This work considers planning problems where a robot must visit a large set of locations to complete a task at each one, for example by cleaning or inspecting them. We show a) how they can be modelled as a finite Markov decision process (MDP), and b) how this model can be simplified to allow for efficient planning in large domains.

ICAR, 2021

A warm-starting approach predicting good initial trajectories for novel situations, and hence speeding up the subsequent trajectory optimization process based on a novel low-dimensional encoding of the environment using discretized signed distance functions (SDF) which are compressed using a tensor train (TT) decomposition approach.
